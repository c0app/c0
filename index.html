Your Training Science
⚡
Progressive Overload
Aim to increase weight by ~2.5% or add 1–2 reps each session. This creates mechanical tension — the primary driver of muscle hypertrophy. Consistent 1% weekly gains compound into ~50% strength increase in a year.
🏃
Running to 5K in 30 min
Current target: 6:00 min/km. Research shows 10% weekly mileage increases are safe. Two runs per week with varied intensity (one easy, one moderate) outperforms two hard runs for aerobic adaptation and injury prevention.
🔥
Strength + Fat Loss
Strength training preserves lean mass during a deficit, which protects your basal metabolic rate. Combined with 2 cardio sessions, research supports 0.5–1% bodyweight fat loss per week without compromising performance.
⚠️
Cost of Skipping
Missing 2+ consecutive strength sessions begins reversing neural adaptations within 5–7 days. Aerobic fitness declines measurably after 10–14 days without running. Consistency beats intensity every time.
😴
Recovery is Training
Muscle protein synthesis peaks 24–48h post-workout. Your rest days are when you actually get stronger. Aim for 7–9h sleep. Oura readiness score below 70 is a real signal to reduce intensity, not push through.
📐
Goal-Action Alignment
Your high-level goal (lean, healthy, 5K) must be matched by your weekly micro-actions. 3 strength + 2 run sessions per week directly maps to your outcome. Logging each session closes the feedback loop between intention and result.
